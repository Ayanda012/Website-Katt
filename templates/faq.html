a panel designed to absorb the sun's rays as a source of energy for generating electricity or heating

Solar panels work by allowing photons, or particles of light, to knock electrons free from atoms, generating a flow of electricity. This electricity is then harnessed and converted into usable power for homes or businesses.

Installing solar panels can lead to significant cost savings on energy bills, reduce reliance on fossil fuels, decrease carbon footprint, and increase the value of your property

Most solar panels come with warranties ranging from 20 to 25 years, but they can last much longer with proper maintenance. Over time, the efficiency of solar panels may decrease slightly, but they typically continue to produce electricity for decades.
