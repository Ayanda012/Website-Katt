Grid-Tied

Grid-tied systems are the most common and cost-effective solar power solution.

They are directly connected to the local electrical grid, allowing any excess power generated during the day to be fed back into the grid.

Through net metering programs, you can receive credits for this excess power, offsetting some of your energy costs.

Cost

Cost on average ranges between R60 000 and over R250 000.

Cost is determined by:

  • Solar Size (in kW)
  • System Type
  • Invertor Type
  • Installation Complexity

Off-Grid

Off-grid solar systems are also common but less prevalent than grid-tied systems. The energy generated is stored in batteries for use during the night or on cloudy days when solar production is low.

They are completely disconnected from the power grid and rely solely on solar power for electricity generation.

Off-grid systems are more expensive than grid-tied systems due to the cost of batteries and additional components required for energy storage.

Hybrid

Hybrid systems combine features of both grid-tied and off-grid systems.

They are connected to the grid but also have battery storage for backup power

These systems generate power during the day, storing excess energy in the battery. If there is excess energy left in the battery, it can be directed back into the grid like grid-tied systems.

During cloudy days or power outages, the stored power can be used to power home appliances.

Rate
